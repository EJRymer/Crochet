To create something using crochet, people use what's called a pattern. It could start something like this:

6sc in MR (6)

Increase around (12)

That may look confusing. But, it is just using everything you already learned to make something.
In a pattern, instead of writing slip stitch or single crochet, we use abbreviations.
(For instance VA instead of Virginia.) Here are some common abbreviations in crochet.

ch= Chain Stitch

sc= Single Crochet

inc= Increase

dec= Decrease

hdc= Half Double Crochet

dc= Double Crochet

BO= bobble stitch

slst= Slip Stitch

MR= Magic Ring

FLO= Front Loops Only (Instead of putting your hook through both loops, you only put it through the front loop.)

BLO= Back Loops Only (Same as FLO, except back loops intsead of front loops.)

So, all 6sc in MR means is: do 6 single crochets in a magic ring.
The number in parentheses after the line just tells you the number of stitches you should end up with after you follow the instructions.
